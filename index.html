Strategy of Local-Distal Points

Local points
Mu points – alarm points
Mu points are more specifically used for Yin disturbances of storing or depletion of energy reserves.
Shu points – associated effect points
Back Shu points are indicated in disturbances of Yang (function in general).
Local points orient the treatment strategy to the affected energetic functional sphere or zone.
Upper: C.V. 17
Middle: C.V. 14, 12, 13
Lower: C.V. 3, 4, 6

Other local points:
K27 – kidney deficiency, asthma
BL 31, 32, 33 – pelvic complaints
Distal points – command points, serve to determine the nature of the energetic manipulation
(tonification, dispersal, warming, cooling, harmonizing)
Five Shu points
Jing–Well
Rong–Spring
Shu–Stream
Jing–River
He–Sea
Source–Luo points; Xi (cleft)
In excess conditions:
– more distal points – dispersing techniques
– less local points – neutral techniques
In deficiency conditions:
– more local points – tonification techniques (warming)
– less distal points – neutral techniques
